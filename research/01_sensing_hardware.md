# 01 — Sensing Hardware: ACS712, ZMPT101B, ESP32 ADC

## ACS712-20A Current Sensor

### Overview
Hall-effect based current sensor. The current-carrying wire passes through the IC's IP+/IP- terminals. The chip outputs an analogue voltage proportional to the magnetic field generated by the current.

- VCC: 5V
- Output at zero current: VCC/2 = 2.5V
- Sensitivity: 100mV/A (20A model), 185mV/A (5A model)
- Maximum output: ~4.5V at full-scale current (20A model)

### Voltage Divider Requirement
ACS712 on 5V → peak output 4.5V → exceeds ESP32 ADC maximum of 3.3V. A voltage divider is **mandatory**.

```
ACS712 VOUT ── [10kΩ] ── GPIO34 ── [22kΩ] ── GND
```

**Design used 22kΩ instead of the spec'd 20kΩ** (unavailable locally). Recalculation:

$$V_{max} = 4.5 \times \frac{22}{10+22} = 3.09V \quad (safe, under 3.3V)$$

$$Sensitivity_{after\ divider} = 100 \times \frac{22}{32} = 68.75\ mV/A$$

$$ADC\ counts/A = \frac{68.75}{3300} \times 4095 \approx 85.4$$

### Critical Wiring Note
The ACS712 measures the magnetic field of the wire passing through it — **the current-carrying conductor must physically route through IP+ and IP-**. Connecting only the VOUT pin does nothing. This was confirmed during Phase 1 testing when `raw=0` was observed despite the sensor being powered, because only the VOUT breakout was connected, not the load wire.

### Zero Calibration
The zero point (2.5V → ~2133 ADC counts after divider) drifts slightly between units and with temperature. Calibration must run at startup with no load and with the output wire already connected to the ADC pin. If the wire is disconnected during calibration, `zero=0` is stored, and all subsequent readings are wildly wrong (23A observed with no load in testing).

## ACS712 Noise Floor (Critical Limitation)

### The Problem
The ESP32's onboard SAR ADC (12-bit, 0–3.3V) has inherent noise of approximately ±20–50 ADC counts across the measurement range. This is a well-documented limitation of the ESP32 ADC.

At 85.4 counts/A, ±50 counts of noise = ±0.58A of apparent current noise.

**Minimum reliably detectable current: ~0.3–0.5A**
**Minimum reliably detectable power at 230V: ~70–115W**

### Observed in Testing
- 25W soldering iron (0.10A): read 0.000A consistently — invisible
- 100W hairdryer mode transitions: occasional spikes visible on mode switch (EMI coupling), not steady reading
- 1000W hairdryer: readable when wire correctly routed through IP+/IP-

### Root Cause
The bottleneck is **the ESP32 ADC, not the ACS712 sensor**. Swapping to the 5A model (185mV/A sensitivity) only improves minimum detection to ~0.13A / ~30W — still far above standby power levels.

### Fix Options

| Option | Min detectable | Additional cost | Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Keep ACS712-20A (current setup) | ~70W | ₹0 | — |
| Swap to ACS712-5A | ~30W | ₹0 (same price) | Low — same wiring |
| ACS712-5A + ADS1115 16-bit ADC | ~2W | ₹180 | Medium — I2C wiring |
| Replace with HLW8012 IC | ~0.1W | ₹80 | Medium — pulse counting |
| Replace with YHDC SCT-013 CT clamp | ~1W | ₹250 | Medium — burden resistor |

**Recommended upgrade path:** HLW8012. Purpose-built for AC energy metering. Used in commercial products (Sonoff POW). Measures true power factor. Pulse output on GPIO — simple interrupt-driven reading. Same ₹80 price.

## ZMPT101B AC Voltage Sensor

### Overview
Voltage transformer + op-amp module. Scales AC mains voltage to a safe analogue signal centered around VCC/2 (~1.65V on 3.3V, or ~2.5V on 5V). Feeds directly into ESP32 ADC without a divider — the op-amp's output swing is limited by the module's design.

- Input: 80–260V AC
- Output: centred AC waveform, safe for 3.3V ADC
- VCC: 5V (module)

### Calibration
ZMPT_VFACTOR converts ADC RMS count → real Vrms. Initial value 0.5 was a guess. Calibrated against a multimeter:

- Serial reading: 286V
- Multimeter reading: 245V
- Calibrated factor: `0.5 × (245/286) = 0.428`

After calibration, readings settled at 238–240V (mains varies). Multimeter agreement within 3%.

### Floating Pin Issue
With ZMPT101B not connected to mains (AC off), GPIO35 floats and ADC reads noise — appeared as ~9.6V in Serial output. Expected behaviour — not a bug.

### Wiring Note
No voltage divider needed. VOUT → GPIO35 direct. GPIO35 is input-only (ADC1_CH7), which also means no risk of accidentally driving output into the sensor.

## ESP32 ADC — General Notes

### Why ADC1 Only (GPIO32–39)
ADC2 is shared with the WiFi RF subsystem. When WiFi is active, ADC2 readings are corrupted or unavailable. GPIO34 and GPIO35 were selected intentionally — both on ADC1.

### Attenuation Setting
`ADC_11db` attenuation used → full 0–3.3V input range, 12-bit resolution (0–4095).

### Nonlinearity
The ESP32 ADC is nonlinear, especially below ~100mV and above ~3.1V. For this project:
- ACS712 zero point sits at ~1.72V after divider → middle of linear range ✓
- ZMPT101B centre sits at ~1.65V → middle of linear range ✓
